Olive Product Design

Olive is an app that I spent designing since the beginning of the year through CareerFoundry. During the project I learned hands on experience in all aspects of UX design. This includes defining the problem, research, development, testing, and the final design of the app. Below is a brief journey through this experience and everything I learned along the way. Before I dove into researching and developing the app, the course tought us a great deal about the process of UX design. For this project, I used the double diamond design process through which I researched, discovered, defined, developed and delivered the final product.

SWOT Analysis

In the beginning stages of development of Olive I conducted a SWOT analysis to uncover opportunities by conducting competitive research. As shown above, one of my primary competitors for Olive is MyFitnessPal. By researching strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats, I was able to hone in on solutions to my problem and provide better ways of solving it.

Affinity Mapping

Following the user interviews, I reviewed the data and constructed an affinity map. This image represents some of the data that was beneficial in creating the user flow for the product.
